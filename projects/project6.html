Project 6: Robotic Car

About

For our sixth project, our professor asked us to build a car which could drive in five preprogrammed motions: forwards, backwards, spin in place, drive in a tight curve, and drive in a large curve. Shown is my simple design along with two programmed motions. To spin in place, I had each wheel turn at the same speed but in opposite directions, and to make an arc I had them both go forwards but at different speeds. The ratio of the speeds of the wheels affects how tight the radius of curvature will be, while the speeds affect how far along the curve the car will go in a given time.
